Our History

The Department of Justice was established to ensure justice, equality, and legal security for all citizens. Over the years, it has played a crucial role in shaping the legal framework of the country.The Department of Justice (DOJ) in India operates under the Ministry of Law and Justice. It has played a crucial role in the country's legal system since India's independence in 1947. Established to oversee judicial appointments, legal reforms, and policy-making. Key role in the appointment of judges to High Courts and the Supreme Court of India. Works towards speedy justice delivery, access to legal aid, and digital transformation of courts. Contributed to judicial modernization, such as e-Courts, online case tracking, and the National Judicial Data Grid (NJDG).

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide fair and accessible justice to every individual while upholding the values of democracy and legal integrity.The Department of Justice aims to uphold the rule of law and strengthen the judicial system in India. Ensure access to justice for all citizens, especially marginalized communities. Support the judiciary by facilitating the appointment of judges and court infrastructure development. Promote legal awareness and digital transformation through initiatives like e-Courts. Implement judicial reforms to ensure speedy, efficient, and transparent justice.
